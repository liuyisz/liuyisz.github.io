Where are Philadelphia’s Section 8 Housings?

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development offers a variety of programs in providing housing assistance for very-low income families, the elderly, and the disabled. Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program is unique because it relies on the private rental market, where private owners are given incentives to rent to lower-income families through assistance payments, pool of families to choose from, and regular inspections to ensure the maintanance of the property. For the tenants, the program promotes freedom of housing choice. Through this system, the program aims to created integrated neighborhoods that include households of a variety of different incomes and backgrounds.

Using big data, we are interested to know the characteristics of neighborhoods that supply Section8 housings in Philadelphia. The characteristics are measured in terms of Income, White Population Percentage, and Voting Pattern. The change is measured through 2 election years, 2012 and 2016. This analysis can be used as a tool for government to evaluate the locations of Section 8 Housings and its neighborhood characteristics, and whether these helps attain the goal of the program. If the goal of Section 8 Program is to integrate the lower income and minority into society as mentioned above, we should see more units in moderate to high income and moderate to low percentage of white population. Being controlled by the private rental market, it will be interesting to see if this is the case. Lastly, this helps to place Section 8 evaluation in the larger landscape of Affordable Housing in Philadelphia.
